In [this exposition](https://happyves.github.io/assets/pdf/blog_matching.pdf), we discuss maximum matching, Hall's theorem and the Gale-Shapeley algorithm.

This is also my first blog post on this site! I may update it if I find the time.

Matching shows up in many problems. How to match employees with jobs such that the most people get a job ? What if we want to take the employees preferences into account ? How to tile a note necessarily square board with dominoes ? 

In this exposition, there isn't much inovation. I think I found a way to describe the blossoms algorithm without using the notion of shrinking graphs.

This post is based on many sources, including class notes, but the two main ones are:

* Chapter 10 of Combinatorial Optimization Algorithms and Complexity by Papadimitriou and Steiglitz
* Chapter 1 of Algorithm Design by Kleinberg and Tardos
